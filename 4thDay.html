Istanbul

Istanbul, the largest city in Turkey, has a history that spans thousands of years. Originally known as Byzantium and later as Constantinople,

Its location between Europe and Asia made it a hub for trade and culture. The city is home to stunning landmarks like Hagia Sophia, the Blue Mosque, and Topkapi Palace, each reflecting its glorious past. Today, Istanbul remains a vibrant city where ancient history meets modern life, attracting millions of visitors every year.

Cappadocia

Cappadocia is one of the most magical regions in central Turkey. It is famous for its unique rock formations and fairy chimneys.

These formations were created by ancient volcanic eruptions. Over time, wind and rain shaped the soft tufa rocks into tall cones. People later carved homes, churches, and tunnels into these rocks. This created an underground world unlike anywhere else on Earth. Cappadocia has been inhabited since ancient times. The Hittites, Persians, Romans, and Byzantines all left their mark here. Early Christians found refuge in its caves and underground cities. They built hidden churches decorated with colorful frescoes. These ancient frescoes still tell stories from the Bible.
